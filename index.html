How KRILL works

Four steps. One clear answer.

When a new token launches, most people drown in charts, claims, and rumors. KRILL compresses that into something you can finish in seconds.

  1. 01

    Scan

    KRILL watches new launches as they appear — metadata, social velocity, liquidity path, holder shape, and public claims — all in one pass.

    • Launch metadata
    • Social velocity
    • Liquidity path
    • Holder signals
  2. 02

    Score

    Everything collapses into a single clarity score from 0–100. High means readable and coherent. Low means confusing or risky.

    • Narrative fit
    • Risk density
    • Liquidity health
    • Claim consistency
  3. 03

    Explain

    No jargon dump. KRILL writes a short plain-English note: what this launch is, why it matters, and what to watch before you decide anything.

    • Plain-language brief
    • Watch items
    • Risk flags
    • What’s missing
  4. 04

    Publish

    Scores and notes go public on the Virtuals launch track. Transparent by default — so the community can check the same signal you see.

    • Public reports
    • Open watchlists
    • Verifiable scores
    • Community updates
